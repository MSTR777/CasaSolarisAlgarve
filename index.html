Around the House

The local guide

Beaches & Swimming

Coves within reach

Burgau beach is an eight-minute walk — sheltered, sandy, quiet out of season. Praia da Luz and its Blue Flag sands are ten minutes by car, and the wilder Cabanas Velhas and Boca do Rio are a short drive west for anyone chasing empty sand.

Hiking & Cliffs

The unbuilt coast

The clifftop path from Burgau to Luz is one of the finest short walks on the coast — an hour each way, all sea. West lies the Costa Vicentina, protected and wild, and the famous Seven Hanging Valleys trail is an easy drive for a bigger day out.

Cycling

Quiet roads inland

The lanes behind Burgau climb gently into cork oak and orchard country, with little traffic and long views back to the sea. Bikes can be hired in Lagos, and the flatter Ecovia coastal route runs east toward Luz and beyond.

Food & the Harbour

Eat where the boats land

The restaurants down by the harbour grill the day's catch and pour local wine without fuss. A handful of family spots in the village stay open year-round — grilled sardines, cataplana, a pastel de nata with your coffee. Book a table on summer evenings.

Day Trips

West to the end of Europe, east to Lagos

Half an hour west, Sagres and Cape St Vincent mark the south-westernmost point of the continent — cliffs, a lighthouse, and Atlantic weather. East, Lagos gives you a walled old town, marina, and the golden coves of Ponta da Piedade. Inland, Monchique's hills bring cooler air and thermal springs. All of it makes an easy day from the door.
